The publication, in one paragraph.

Tokenized Report is a weekly editorial newsletter and reference library covering real-world asset tokenization, stablecoin yield, and the regulatory and technical infrastructure being built underneath digital ownership. It is written for the women, founders, advisors, and operators who need a serious, independent read on the parts of crypto that institutional capital is actually deploying into. No price predictions. No paid placements. No bro energy.

Why this publication exists.

I have been in crypto since 2018 and watching the real-world asset space specifically for the past three years, since BlackRock filed for its first tokenized treasury fund and the conversation inside traditional finance quietly changed from "if" to "when." Tokenization is the part of crypto that survives the speculation cycle. It is the part that connects to housing, treasuries, private credit, and the regulated financial system that most people actually use.

The problem: the publications covering this space write for either of two audiences. They write for retail crypto traders who want price action, or they write for institutional analysts who already speak in basis points and SEC release numbers. There is no serious, accessible publication for the people in between: the founders, advisors, family office staff, women building wealth in digital assets, and operators who need to understand what is actually happening without being condescended to or shilled at.

That is the gap Tokenized Report fills.

"Tokenization is the part of crypto that survives the speculation cycle."

What you can expect.

One newsletter per week, every Sunday. Three stories, one chart, one tactic. Sent before the market opens Monday so it is in your inbox when you start the week, not when you are mid-task on Tuesday.

An open library of reference material. The cheatsheets at /library/ are free, downloadable as PDFs, and built to be used. The Tokenized Asset Vetting Checklist, the Red-Flag Phrases list, the Glossary, the Chain Comparison, and the Q1 2026 Numbers. These are the documents I wish had existed when I started covering this space.

Sourcing you can verify. Every number, every quote, every regulatory claim is linked to a primary source. SEC filings. Fund prospectuses. On-chain data with the contract address. Not aggregator headlines. Not "according to sources." Real receipts.

Background.

I am the founder of Women in Crypto Global, an independent publication for women in crypto that I started in 2021. I hold an MBA. I built Tokenized Report as a focused vertical inside WICG because the RWA story deserved its own publication with its own editorial voice and its own reference library, separate from the broader crypto coverage WICG does for a general women's audience.

I am not a financial advisor. I do not hold positions in any of the tokenized products this publication covers, and I do not accept compensation, equity, or affiliate relationships from any project, exchange, or protocol that appears in editorial coverage. Sponsorships and partnerships, when they exist, are disclosed inline and never influence reporting. The full policy is on the Editorial Standards page.

Editorial independence.

Tokenized Report does not run sponsored content disguised as editorial. It does not take affiliate compensation from exchanges, custodians, or token issuers. It does not have undisclosed equity stakes in companies it covers. When the publication eventually accepts sponsorships, they will appear in clearly labeled sections that are visually distinct from editorial content, and the sponsor relationship will be disclosed in the issue and on the website.

If you find an error in published reporting, the correction policy is simple: email hello@womenincryptoglobal.com with the issue, the URL, and the source for the correction. Verified errors are corrected within 48 hours with a visible correction note appended to the article or issue.
