Topics We Cover

The financial infrastructure being built on-chain.

Four beats, covered weekly. The technology, the policy, and the practical implications for readers building wealth.

Beat One

Real-World Assets (RWA)

Tokenized Treasuries, real estate, private credit, and commodities. The institutional asset classes moving on-chain through issuers like BlackRock, Franklin Templeton, Apollo, and Fidelity. As of Q1 2026, the tokenized RWA market exceeds $19 billion and continues to grow at triple-digit annual rates.

Beat Two

Stablecoin Yield

USDC, USDT, and the regulatory fight over whether stablecoin holders can earn yield. The GENIUS Act of 2025 reshaped the stablecoin landscape. The CLARITY Act may reshape it again. We cover the policy, the products, and what each shift means for retail holders.

Beat Three

Tokenization Structure

The legal, custodial, and technical mechanics of putting financial instruments on-chain. SPVs and bankruptcy remoteness. Proof of reserves. Smart contract audits. Frameworks for evaluating what you are actually being offered when an issuer pitches a tokenized product.

Beat Four

Policy and Regulation

SEC tokenization guidance. The CLARITY Act through Congress. CFTC jurisdiction. State-level legislation. Plus the ongoing DTC tokenization pilot and what it means for traditional securities settlement. The policy moves that determine what is possible in tokenization.

Frequently Asked

Questions about Tokenized Report.

What is Tokenized Report?

Tokenized Report is a weekly editorial newsletter covering real-world asset tokenization, stablecoin yield, and on-chain financial infrastructure. It is published by Women in Crypto Global and written for readers who want serious financial journalism applied to the crypto sector, without the hype or speculation that dominates most crypto media.

Who is Tokenized Report for?

Tokenized Report is built for women who hold the largest share of new US crypto wallets in 2026, for financial advisors fielding client questions about tokenized assets, for journalists covering the space, and for any reader who wants to understand the financial infrastructure being built on-chain without learning to read smart contract code.

How often does Tokenized Report publish?

Tokenized Report publishes weekly on Sunday mornings. Each issue follows a consistent format: three stories, one chart, and one tactical takeaway, designed to be read in seven to ten minutes.

Does Tokenized Report cost anything?

The weekly newsletter is free. All editorial guides in the Tokenized Report Library are also free with no email required. A paid tier with extended analysis, premium guides, and direct access to the editor will launch later in 2026.

Who writes Tokenized Report?

Tokenized Report is edited by Julie James, founder of Women in Crypto Global. Julie launched WICG in 2021 and rebuilt it as an editorial publication in 2026 to serve the audience traditional crypto media has overlooked.
